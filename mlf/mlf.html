About This Subject

Machine Learning Foundations (MLF) is a subject that builds the mathematical and statistical foundation required to understand and implement machine learning algorithms. Before you can understand how algorithms like neural networks or support vector machines work, you need a strong grasp of the underlying mathematics.

This subject covers three key areas — calculus for understanding gradients and optimization, linear algebra for working with data in matrix form, and probability theory for building probabilistic models. These are not just academic topics — they are the actual tools used by data scientists and machine learning engineers at companies like Google, Meta, and Amazon every day.

These week-wise notes are designed for students of IIT Madras BS in Data Science, NPTEL courses, BCA, MCA, and B.Tech programs who want to build a rock-solid mathematical foundation before diving into advanced machine learning techniques.

Week 2 – Calculus

Review of essential calculus concepts for machine learning — derivatives, partial derivatives, chain rule, and gradients. Understanding how calculus is used in gradient descent to minimize loss functions. Multivariate calculus and its role in training machine learning models efficiently.

View

Week 3 – Linear Algebra - Least Squares Regression

Understanding vectors, matrices, and matrix operations. Introduction to least squares regression — the fundamental technique for fitting a line through data points. How linear algebra helps solve regression problems analytically using the normal equation and matrix decomposition.

View

Week 4 – Linear Algebra - Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Understanding eigenvalues and eigenvectors — one of the most important concepts in linear algebra for machine learning. How they are computed, their geometric interpretation, and their applications in dimensionality reduction, covariance matrices, and principal component analysis (PCA).

View

Week 5 – Linear Algebra - Symmetric Matrices

Properties of symmetric matrices and why they appear so frequently in machine learning. Understanding positive definite and positive semi-definite matrices. Spectral decomposition of symmetric matrices and how they relate to covariance matrices used in multivariate statistics and ML algorithms.

View

Week 6 – SVD and Principal Component Analysis in Image Processing

Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) — one of the most powerful tools in data science. Understanding how SVD relates to PCA and how both are used for dimensionality reduction. Practical application of PCA in image compression and noise reduction with real examples from image processing.

View

Week 7 – Unconstrained Optimisation

Understanding optimization as the heart of machine learning training. Gradient descent and its variants — batch, stochastic, and mini-batch gradient descent. How learning rate affects convergence. Second-order optimization methods like Newton's method and their advantages over first-order methods.

View

Week 8 – Convex Sets, Functions, and Optimisation Problems

Understanding convexity and why it matters in optimization. Properties of convex sets and convex functions. How convex optimization guarantees finding the global minimum. Applications of convex optimization in support vector machines, LASSO regression, and other important ML algorithms.

View

Week 9 – Constrained Optimisation and Lagrange Multipliers

How to solve optimization problems with constraints using Lagrange multipliers. Understanding the KKT (Karush-Kuhn-Tucker) conditions. Logistic regression formulated as a constrained optimization problem. How these mathematical tools are used to derive support vector machine classifiers.

View

Week 10 – Examples of Probabilistic Models in Machine Learning

Introduction to probability theory for machine learning — random variables, probability distributions, Bayes theorem, and conditional probability. How probabilistic models like Naive Bayes, Gaussian mixture models, and hidden Markov models are used to make predictions under uncertainty.

View

Week 11 – Exponential Family of Distributions

Understanding the exponential family of probability distributions — Gaussian, Bernoulli, Poisson, and Gamma distributions. Why the exponential family is central to generalized linear models. How understanding these distributions helps in choosing the right model for different types of data.

View

Week 12 – Parameter Estimation and Expectation Maximization

Maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) and maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation for fitting probability distributions to data. The Expectation Maximization (EM) algorithm — a powerful iterative method for finding parameters in models with hidden variables. Applications in clustering and mixture models.

Why Study Machine Learning Foundations?

Machine Learning is one of the most exciting and fast-growing fields in technology. But to truly understand how ML algorithms work — and to be able to build, debug, and improve them — you need a strong mathematical foundation. That is exactly what this subject provides.

Without understanding calculus, you cannot understand how gradient descent trains a neural network. Without linear algebra, you cannot understand how PCA reduces dimensions or how SVD compresses data. Without probability, you cannot build models that make reliable predictions under uncertainty.

These notes are ideal for students of IIT Madras BS in Data Science, NPTEL machine learning courses, and anyone preparing for a career in data science, artificial intelligence, or machine learning engineering at top companies.
