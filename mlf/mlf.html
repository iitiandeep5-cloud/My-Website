About This Subject

Machine Learning Foundations (MLF) is a subject that builds the mathematical and statistical foundation required to understand and implement machine learning algorithms. Before you can understand how algorithms like neural networks or support vector machines work, you need a strong grasp of the underlying mathematics.

This subject covers three key areas — calculus for understanding gradients and optimization, linear algebra for working with data in matrix form, and probability theory for building probabilistic models. These are not just academic topics — they are the actual tools used by data scientists and machine learning engineers at companies like Google, Meta, and Amazon every day.

These week-wise notes are designed for students of IIT Madras BS in Data Science, NPTEL courses, BCA, MCA, and B.Tech programs who want to build a rock-solid mathematical foundation before diving into advanced machine learning techniques.

Why Study Machine Learning Foundations?

Machine Learning is one of the most exciting and fast-growing fields in technology. But to truly understand how ML algorithms work — and to be able to build, debug, and improve them — you need a strong mathematical foundation. That is exactly what this subject provides.

Without understanding calculus, you cannot understand how gradient descent trains a neural network. Without linear algebra, you cannot understand how PCA reduces dimensions or how SVD compresses data. Without probability, you cannot build models that make reliable predictions under uncertainty.

These notes are ideal for students of IIT Madras BS in Data Science, NPTEL machine learning courses, and anyone preparing for a career in data science, artificial intelligence, or machine learning engineering at top companies.
